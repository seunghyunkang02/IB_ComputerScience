    Computer Aided Design (CAD)

    • Definition
      • CAD is a computer technology that designs a product and documents.

    • Used in
      • Architecture
      • Engineering
      • Drafting
      • Others to creating precision drawings or technical illustrations

    • Uses
      • Transfer detailed diagrams of product's materials, processes, tolerances and dimensions with specific conventions for the product.

    • Key Features
      • Can produce either two-dimensional or three-dimensional diagrams
      • Can design from any angles, even from inside looking out
      • Simulation of designs without building a physical prototype
      • Interfaces with machines to design
      • Can re-use the designs
      • Photorealistic rendering

    • Advantages of CAD
      • More accurate than hand-drawn designs - reduces human error
      • Saves time in order to modify existing ideas or designs
      • Allowing rapid prototyping

    Mail Merge

    How to Mail Merge with Word and Excel:
    Step 1: Open Excel and type your email recipient's name and their subjects and their levels.


    Step 2: Open Word and click Mailings then 'Start Mail Merge'-->'E-mail Messages'


    Step 3: Click 'Select Recipients' then 'Use an Existing List' to connect the Word document and the spreadsheet from Excel.


    Step 4: Type your email to the recipients on your word document, make sure you put the data properly by clicking 'Insert Merge Field'.



    Step 5: You can click 'Preview Results' to preview your mail merge.


    Last step: If you are finished, click 'Finish & Merge' then 'Send Email Messages' to send your messages to your recipients.
